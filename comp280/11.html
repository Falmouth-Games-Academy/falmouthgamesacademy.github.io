Lecture:
This week introduces the final specialism of this module: Interfaces and Interaction. As a specialism, Interfaces and Interaction is heavily embedded in field of study known as Human-computer interaction (HCI). According to the ACM definition HCI is, "a discipline concerned with the design, evaluation and implementation of interactive computing systems for human use." This week's lecture will provide a general introduction to HCI.

Workshops:

The first workshop this week will focus on the design and prototyping of user interfaces. The practical aspects of the workshop will be informed by a short introduction to Donald Norman's, Psychopathology of Everyday Things. We will also review the five principles of Gestalt Theory and use them as a means of creating order and helping users better understand out UI design.

The second workshop will focus on the practical aspects of creating UI in Unreal Engine and Unity. You will be introduced to the various tools for creating UI and tasked with implementing your designs from the previous workshop.


Learning Objectives:
  • Explain the importance of placing the user at the center of the design process
  • Demonstrate a knowledge of the some core design principles of user-centered design
  • Implement basic UI in Unreal or Unity
