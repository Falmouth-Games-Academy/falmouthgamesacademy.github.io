The introduction this week, will outline the various assignment briefs and how they map to the module structure. This is a chance for you to ask questions about the scope and possible outcomes of the module. The rest of the session will be split into two:

The first half of this session introduces human-centred design principles with a focus on Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR). This discussion will aim to frame the importance of both qualitative and quantitative research as an invaluable feedback mechanism for iteratively improving the product. Fundamentally, it is about keeping the user's values and needs at the heart of the development process.

The second part of the session will focus on the practical aspects of documenting your research process. You will be tasked with setting up a Github pages site, where all information relating to your research can be hosted. The site map needs to contain placeholders for:

  • Introduction
  • Qualitative Research
  • Quantitative Research
  • Discussion
  • Conclusion


Learning Objectives:
  • Explain the importance of placing the user at the center of the design process
  • Briefly describe and compare different user-centered design research methods
  • Demonstrate a knowledge of the core principles of user-centered design
  • List and describe the key components that make up the hardware side of reality systems.
