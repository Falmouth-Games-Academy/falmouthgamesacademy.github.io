This week we investigate the psychological and physiological factors involved in making users feel 'present' within virtual worlds

Lecture:

When it comes to virtual worlds, positioned anywhere along the reality continuum, the main aim is to facilitate an environment where the user forgets that the experience is in anyway mediated by technology. This might seem like a beg challenge but luckily, humans are extremely good at suspending disbelief. There are many psychological factors at play and this week we will deep dive into the psychology of feeling present in virtual worlds.

Workshop:

Taking into consideration the discussions from week 1, we will explore various forms of locomotion and player perspective within immersive environments. We will also experiment with some passive haptics and monitor their effectiveness.


Learning Objectives:
  • Discuss the term 'presence' relative to AR/VR system design
  • Outline Gestalts Psychology
  • Explore the ways in which the mind can be tricked into believing illusions
