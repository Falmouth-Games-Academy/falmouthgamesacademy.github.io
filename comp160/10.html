Software quality is of utmost quality in the development of digital games. Simply put, players will not tolerate bugs. Fixing bugs is expensive, and incrasingly so as projects scale in size and development time. It is, therefore, of utmost and critical importance to apply appropriate software engineering techniques to analyse and verify the quality of source code. This session reviews several testing techniques and encourages reflection on the software engineering approaches applied throughout the collaborative game development project.


Studio Practice

Each week you will engage in mandatory studio practice under the supervision of mentors who will help you to develop your collaborative programming practice. You are also expected to attend a mandatory meeting with your product owner each week.


Workshop
Learning Objectives:
  • Explain unit testing techniques, such as mutation testing and static analysis
  • Apply unit testing techiques to a live software development project
  • Evaluate the merits and flaws of your existing software engineering practice
