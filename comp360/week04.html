This session will introduce how to quantize your data, select appropriate statistical tests, determine the minimum required sample size, and interpret the outcome of tests, These will build upon your knowledge of R, a free software package for statistical computing. Statistical analysis of empirical data is an essential part of almost every research project, and so knowledge of the appropriate tools and techniques is vital to disseminating your research findings.

Learning Objectives:

By the end of this session, you should be able to:

  • Explain the link between making inferences and statistical analysis
  • Explain the foundations of null-hypothesis significance testing
  • Outline the importance of validity and reliability in data analysis
  • Suggest the most appropriate statistical analysis to apply to given data
  • Describe how to conduct a power analysis to determine minimal sample sizes for common data analysis techniques
This will prepare you to:
  • Analyse data using R
  • Interpret and explain inferences that can be made from the output of statistical tests conducted in R
  • Utilise computational statistics in your research
