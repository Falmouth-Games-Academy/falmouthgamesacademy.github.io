This session covers how to write with clarity and authority, revisting material on scholarship and academic writing. A dissection of publication resulting from an undergraduate final year project provides a benchmark and constast against which you can analyse your own work and writing ability. Hopefully inspiring fervour and aspiration. It then goes on to formalize the management of the software development life cycle. A clear, detailed, and justifiably valid approach is critical to the integrity of the claims made in the dissertation, and so this builds on research philosophy to forms a core foundation to the research project and eventual dissemination of your work.

Learning Objectives:

By the end of this session, you should be able to:

  • Reflect upon and recall how to communicate effectively in an academic style
  • Evaluate the characteristics of academic writing
  • Analyse system development life-cycles your approach to the final-year project
  • Position an argument about development lifecycles in an appropriate scholarly manner
