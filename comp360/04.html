This session covers the expectations that Falmouth University and the British Computer Society have in respect to software quality and how you assure quality in your final-year project. The final-year project epitomizes your ability to deliver a substantial piece of software as an individual endeavor, and as such presents a key point at which you demonstrate your learning of computing fundamentals. Although many of you will already be testing your software as a matter of practice, and there will be variations in your approach depending on your project, this session outlines how to demonstrate and evidence your software testing process.

Learning Objectives:

By the end of this session, you should be able to:

  • Discuss the role of quality in software development
  • Explain how to assure and control the quality of software and of research
  • Demonstrate the ways in which you have tested your software
This will prepare you to:
  • Show how you conducted validation, verification, and testing of your software
  • Write up your testing and refactoring process
