This session explores how realistic surface and lighting effects are achieved in 3D graphics. Just as in photography or film, illumination in graphics is often the key to achieving a desired aesthetic.

Learning Objectives:
  • Explain the Blinn-Phong illumination model
  • Implement Blinn-Phong illumination in your own programs
  • Describe how effects such as normal mapping can be used to render realistic materials
