This session introduces numerical methods for simulating physical systems. Computers are discrete: they operate in fixed time steps. The physical universe, by contrast, is continuous, and thus can never be simulated perfectly. Understanding numerical methods is important in ensuring that simulations are sufficiently accurate and do not suffer from numerical instabilities which can cause glitches.

Learning Objectives:
  • Recall the key concepts of numerical methods
  • Explain why numerical instabilities can arise in physical simulations
  • Write programs that use advanced numerical methods
